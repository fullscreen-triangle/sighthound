About This Framework

S-Entropy Coordinates: A three-dimensional bounded metric space [0,1]³ representing kinetic entropy (Sk), temporal entropy (St), and energetic entropy (Se). This coordinate system encodes intrinsic distance relationships without pre-computed lookup tables.

Key Innovation: Distance computation is O(1) and independent of database size, reducing storage from petabytes to megabytes while maintaining metric accuracy.

Applications:

  • Shortest Path: SEBD (S-Entropy Bidirectional Dijkstra) algorithm finds optimal routes by bidirectional search in 3D coordinate space
  • Mobility Analysis: Calculate reachable regions based on motion constraints and speed limits
  • Satellite Coverage: Determine line-of-sight accessibility to orbital infrastructure

Theoretical Foundation: Based on the Dual-Domain Execution Model which combines temporal programming (deterministic execution) with frequency-domain virtual substates (rich computation), mediated by mean-recovery constraints.

Mathematical References

Intrinsic Metric Structure

S-entropy coordinates form a natural metric space where distances are computed directly from coordinates without pre-computed graphs or lookup tables.

SEBD Algorithm

S-Entropy Bidirectional Dijkstra searches forward through time-domain subtasks and backward through frequency-domain virtual substates.

Mean-Recovery Constraint

All virtual decompositions satisfy the constraint that their mean equals the physical observable state, ensuring global coherence.

Coordinate-Free Navigation

No absolute positioning required; only relative metric relationships between locations matter for pathfinding and distance calculation.
